Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about AI consulting and working with Stephanie Culver Advisory.

What does an AI business consultant do?

An AI business consultant identifies where artificial intelligence can solve real operational problems — not just where it sounds interesting. This means auditing existing workflows, pinpointing high-value AI use cases, and then guiding implementation from vendor selection through employee adoption. The engagement ends with a measurable business outcome, not a slide deck. At Stephanie Culver Advisory, every engagement starts with the question: "What would have to be true for AI to pay for itself here?"

Project engagements for a focused AI workflow audit and implementation roadmap typically range from $5,000–$15,000 depending on scope and company size. Fractional AI officer retainers start at $2,500 per month for eight hours of strategic advisory time. What kinds of business problems can AI solve?

AI is most effective at solving problems that involve repetitive decisions, large volumes of text or data, or tasks where speed and consistency matter more than human judgment. Common examples include automating customer support ticket routing, extracting data from documents and contracts, generating first-draft marketing copy, predicting inventory needs, and flagging anomalies in financial records. If a task involves pattern recognition, summarization, or classification — and your team does it hundreds of times per week — AI is almost certainly a fit.

How do I know if my business is ready for AI?

Your business is ready for AI if you have at least one workflow that is repeated frequently, follows recognizable patterns, and currently requires manual time that could be better spent elsewhere. You do not need a large data science team or sophisticated IT infrastructure — most AI implementations today use cloud-based tools that connect to systems you already have. A readiness assessment with Stephanie Culver Advisory takes about 90 minutes and produces a clear recommendation on AI viability for your top three use cases.

What industries does Stephanie Culver Advisory work with?

Stephanie Culver Advisory works with service businesses, operations-heavy companies, and professional services firms — including logistics, healthcare administration, legal, real estate, and B2B SaaS. The common thread is not the industry but the problem: teams spending significant time on work that AI can do faster and more consistently. Stephanie does not specialize in consumer apps or hardware manufacturing; the sweet spot is a business where employees are buried in repeatable, process-driven work.

How long does an AI implementation project take?

A focused AI implementation — from kickoff to a live, working solution — typically takes 6–12 weeks depending on the complexity of the workflow and the systems involved. A simple document automation or chatbot project can go live in 4 weeks; a multi-system integration with change management takes 10–16 weeks. Stephanie Culver Advisory does not do multi-year transformation programs — the goal is a working solution in the client's hands, fast.

What is a fractional AI officer?

A fractional AI officer is an experienced AI strategist who works with your company on a part-time retainer basis, providing the strategic leadership of a full-time Chief AI Officer at a fraction of the cost. This typically includes 8–20 hours per month of advisory time: reviewing AI opportunities, evaluating vendors, guiding internal teams, and keeping leadership informed on developments that affect the business. It is most useful for companies that need ongoing AI direction but are not yet ready to hire a full-time AI executive.
